The Five Observable States

These states describe what a teacher observes about a student's apparent readiness and well-being in the classroom...

1 — Engaged and Ready

The student appears calm, focused, and ready to learn.

2 — Mostly Ready

The student is present and compliant but may appear tired or quietly disengaged.

3 — Mildly Unsettled

The student shows mild signs of distraction, restlessness, or low-level friction.

4 — Struggling

The student is visibly dysregulated — withdrawn, upset, acting out, or unavailable for learning.

5 — Distressed

The student is in significant distress.
