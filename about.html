About

How it works

Most educational software tools depend on internet connections, accounts, and the collection of user data. Many lock features behind paid tiers, interrupt the classroom experience with advertisements, or are hosted by unknown third parties with unclear data practices. Simple Ed Tools takes a different approach where users and their data privacy is important.

When you open one of the tools here, everything happens locally on your device. Your flashcards, your notes, your work — none of it leaves your computer. When you close the tab, it is gone unless you choose to save it yourself.

These tools are built as single HTML files and are quite small in size. That means you can open them in a browser, save them to your desktop, share them with a colleague, or use them securely offline. There is no installation, subscription, or account needed. They are always free to use and have no ads.

Why single files?

Apps that fit into a single file are easy to understand and work with, even on underpowered devices. Their code can be inspected and modified by anyone, and they work the same on any computer with a browser. Every tool on this site is built that way. Each one is a single HTML file containing everything it needs to run, with no external dependencies, no required internet connection, and no hidden processes running in the background.

Who made this?

Simple Ed Tools was built by Michael Littrell, an educator and professor at Tennessee Tech University. These tools grew out of a need for simple, reliable utilities that work in any classroom environment regardless of network access or budget constraints.

Privacy

The tools at Simple Ed Tools collect nothing. No accounts are required, no analytics run in the background, and nothing you do is ever transmitted anywhere. What you create and work with stays entirely on your device.
